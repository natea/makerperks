# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who build things and want to claim the free credits, perks, and discounts
available to them — but don't know what they qualify for or where to redeem it:

- **Startups / founders** — claiming cloud, AI, and infra credits to extend runway.
- **Students** — developer tools, learning, and design perks via .edu/SheerID.
- **OSS maintainers** — free tiers and grants for public projects.
- **Indie developers** — generous free tiers and solo-builder programs.
- **Non-profits / NGOs** — 501(c)(3)-gated software via TechSoup and vendor programs.
- **AI agents** — fetching the directory as structured data (`llms.txt`, `perks.json`).

Context: someone mid-build, time-poor, often acting on a viral "$800K in credits"
post with no idea which programs are real, current, or worth the application effort.
They arrive asking one question: **"I am _X_ — what can I claim, how much, and
where do I redeem it?"**

## Product Purpose

MakerPerks is the single trustworthy, browseable, agent-friendly directory of
builder perks. It exists because the offers are scattered across viral LinkedIn
posts and unmaintained `awesome-*` GitHub lists — so most builders leave $100K+
unclaimed. Success looks like: a builder picks their persona, scans the highest-value
programs they actually qualify for, sees honest eligibility/caveats and a verified
date, and clicks straight through to redeem. The directory is a utility people
bookmark and return to, and a data source agents can consume directly.

Scope is deliberately fenced to **builder + dev-adjacent** perks (cloud, AI/ML, dev
tools, SaaS, infra, security, data, plus learning, design, productivity, hardware).
Pure consumer/lifestyle discounts (fashion, travel, streaming, banking) are out — a
sharp boundary is part of the trust.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy & precise.** Calm, credible, accurate. Founders claim real money
through this, so it reads like a reliable reference, not a hype machine. Voice is
plain-language and low-jargon — welcoming to a first-time student, not just a
VC-backed insider. We state amounts honestly (ranges, "up to", caveats), surface
freshness, and never oversell. Three words: **dependable, clear, current.**

## Anti-references

This must NOT look like any of:

- **Generic SaaS-cream AI slop** — cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, identical
  icon-card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows, the hero-metric template. The
  default AI look.
- **A plain markdown awesome-list** — an unstyled GitHub README dumped to a page
  (the source lists, cloudcredits.io's plainness): no hierarchy, a wall of links.
- **A cluttered coupon/deals site** — RetailMeNot/Honey energy: ad-dense, spammy,
  banner-heavy. The opposite of our no-ads, no-affiliate ethos.
- **Stiff corporate enterprise** — navy-and-gray B2B template, stock-photo
  corporate, lifeless and forgettable.

## Design Principles

1. **Persona-first.** "Who are you?" is the spine of the experience, not a filter
   bolted on. Everything orients around getting a builder to the perks they qualify
   for, fast.
2. **Trust is the product.** Verified dates, honest eligibility and caveats, no
   affiliate links or ads. Credibility — not breadth or hype — is what earns the
   bookmark.
3. **Built to scan and compare.** A directory's job is fast decision-making. Show
   enough (value, audience, effort, freshness) to decide without a click; reserve
   depth for the detail page.
4. **Agent-native by design.** The structured data and agent outputs (`llms.txt`,
   `perks.json`, JSON-LD) are first-class deliverables, generated from the same
   source of truth, never an afterthought.
5. **Curated, not exhaustive.** The scope fence is a feature. A sharp, opinionated
   boundary (builder + dev-adjacent) beats a bloated everything-list and protects
   the directory's identity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast (≥3:1 large), full keyboard
navigation, visible focus states, semantic structure, and a
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Color is never the sole
carrier of meaning (status/region/audience use text or icon, not just hue). Plain
language throughout, for a broad audience that includes students and first-time
founders.
